UCLAN Cyprus

The conference will be hosted at the University of Central Lancashire Cyprus (UCLan Cyprus), located in Pyla, in the Larnaca district. Established in 2012, UCLan Cyprus is the first British university campus on the island, combining the academic standards of the UK higher-education system with a modern Mediterranean setting. The campus features contemporary lecture theatres, seminar rooms, a spacious auditorium, and dedicated networking areas, providing an ideal environment for academic exchange and collaboration. UCLan Cyprus is situated approximately 15 minutes from Larnaka International Airport and is easily accessible via major transport routes. The campus lies close to the coastline, offering visiting delegates a welcoming and pleasant environment with nearby accommodation options, caf√©s, and beach-side facilities. With a blend of academic excellence, modern infrastructure, and convenient travel connections, UCLan Cyprus offers an exceptional venue for our conference.



Village of Pyla

Pyla is a historic village northeast of Larnaka with continuous habitation since the Early and Middle Bronze Age (2400‚Äì1700 B.C.), confirmed by archaeological excavations. During medieval times, it was initially a private fief and later a royal estate, featuring a defensive tower that formed part of the Larnaka bay observatory network. The village later came under Venetian rule and was taken over by the Ottomans in 1570‚Äì71, becoming and remaining a mixed community of Greek Cypriots and Turkish Cypriots.



The name Pyla is believed either to derive from the Greek word pyli (gate), referring to its strategic location leading to the Mesaoria plain, or to share origins with ancient Greek toponyms related to Achaean settlement. Today, visitors can explore medieval and cultural landmarks, including the tower, a folk art museum, a Turkish Cypriot mosque, and other heritage sites. Population records from 1881 to 2001 show steady growth, with both Greek Cypriot and Turkish Cypriot communities residing in the village throughout its history.
